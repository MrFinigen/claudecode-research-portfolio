· Case Study 01

CommBank App —
Cards Experience

Uplifting the 'Cards' section of the CommBank App to serve users' needs using a mixed-methods research approach — from information architecture to usability validation.

Organisation
Commonwealth Bank of Australia
My Role
Lead UX Researcher
Timeline
2021 – 2022
Methods
Mixed Methods
Overview

The challenge

The CommBank App's Cards section had grown organically over time — resulting in an experience that didn't reflect what customers actually needed or how they thought about their cards. The goal was to design a Cards experience that was truly user-centred: surfacing the right tasks, in the right structure, at the right time.

Discovery

Research questions

What do users currently do in the Cards section — and what do they expect to be able to do?

Which unimportant tasks are being overserved and which important tasks are being under-served?

What does an optimal information architecture for a Cards page look like — carousel vs. wallet, single vs. multiple pages?

Will users be able to complete their card-related tasks in a brand new Cards section?

Approach

Research methods

🔍
Desktop Research
Competitive analysis and audit of existing Cards features to identify patterns and gaps.
📊
Quantitative Top Tasks
Large-scale survey measuring task importance vs. difficulty to prioritise the IA redesign.
🃏
Card Sorting
Open and closed card sorts to understand how users naturally group card-related tasks.
💡
Concept Testing
Qualitative sessions comparing carousel, wallet, and hybrid IA concepts with real customers.
🧪
Usability Testing
Moderated prototype testing to validate the final design before build commenced.
