Case Study 02

CommBank —
Bank Vault Experience

Informing the future of safe deposit boxes and bank vaults at CommBank for both customers and staff — through in-depth interviews, ethnographic research, and employee research.

Organisation
Commonwealth Bank of Australia
My Role
Lead UX Researcher
Timeline
2023 – 2024
Methods
Qualitative, Ethnographic
Overview

The challenge

Safe deposit boxes and bank vaults are an under-researched part of the CommBank customer experience. With no clear digital strategy in place for this service, there was an opportunity to understand both customer and staff experiences end-to-end — and to chart a path toward a modern, frictionless experience that preserved what customers valued most: security and peace of mind.

Discovery

Research questions

What are the barriers and drivers to customers obtaining a safe custody facility with CommBank?

What are the current state pain-points, opportunities, and gaps in the safe custody experience?

What are customers' highest priorities when it comes to safe custody — and what does trust look like in this context?

What aspects of the staff experience are most burdensome, and how can we streamline them?

Approach

Research methods

🔍
Desktop Research
Audit of existing safe custody processes, industry comparisons, and internal data analysis.
🗣️
In-Depth Interviews
Deep qualitative conversations with customers to uncover motivations, mental models, and unmet needs.
👁️
Ethnographic Research
Observational field research in CommBank branches to understand the real-world safe custody process.
👥
Employee Research
Interviews and walkthroughs with branch staff to surface operational pain-points and improvement opportunities.
Findings

Key insights & design principles

🔒
Design Principle 1: Secure
The primary driver for customers is peace of mind — the certainty that their valuables are kept safe. Every touchpoint must reinforce trust and control.
⚡
Design Principle 2: Frictionless
Customers and staff are burdened by manual, paper-based processes. The experience must remove effort without compromising the sense of security.
1

Enable customers to book appointments, manage fees, and view facility accounts directly in the CommBank app.

2

Streamline the in-branch process through unified digital systems, availability management, and elimination of physical forms.

3

Integrate a fully digital onboarding and access hub in CommSee (CommBank's enterprise system) to remove staff burden.

Artefacts

Research artefacts

Outcome

What happened next

Research directly shaped the product roadmap

Following the delivery of research findings, CommBank initiated development of a customer-centred self-service portal within the CommBank app — enabling appointment booking, fee management, and facility account visibility. Simultaneously, a fully integrated onboarding and access hub was scoped for CommSee, CommBank's enterprise system, to eliminate staff burden and unify the in-branch experience. Both initiatives were scheduled to launch in early 2025.
